## üßæ **Project Overview**
This project involved building a complete **Retail Inventory Management Database System** to help a clothing retailer overcome challenges with manual inventory tracking, stock inaccuracies, and customer lookup issues.

The final system handles:
- Product inventory  
- Stock location mapping  
- Customer information & preferences  
- Sales orders & order items  
- Store details  
- Purchase orders & procurement tracking  

The goal was to create a **fully normalized relational schema** that improves data integrity, enhances store operations, and enables reliable decision-making.

---

## üìä **Key Findings**
- Manual tracking caused frequent **stock inaccuracies**, slow service, and customer dissatisfaction.  
- Inventory needed to be tied to **aisle, bin, shelf, and floor** to allow precise in-store item lookup.  
- Analysis showed major redundancy across product, order, and customer information.  
- Normalization to **3NF** significantly improved data accuracy and eliminated duplicate records.  
- Linking products to locations, orders, and stores enabled **end-to-end visibility** across all retail operations.  
- Incorporating purchase orders enabled the store to better manage **replenishment and vendor relations**.
